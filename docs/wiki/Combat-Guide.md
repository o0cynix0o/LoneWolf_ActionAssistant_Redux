# Combat Guide

The Combat tab tracks fights using Lone Wolf Book 1 rules.

## Combat Skill

Your current Combat Skill starts from your Book 1 character roll and changes when the book or your equipment changes it.

Combat can also include temporary modifiers:

- no Weapon penalty
- Weaponskill bonus
- Mindblast bonus when the enemy is not immune
- enemy-specific modifiers
- timed modifiers for special fights

## Starting A Fight

When a section has an audited combat preset, use the combat button in the section controls. The preset fills in the enemy Combat Skill, Endurance, immunity notes, evasion rules, victory routes, and any special conditions that are known.

You can also start a fight manually from the Combat tab or CLI.

## Combat Rounds

Each round uses a random number from 0 to 9 and the Combat Results Table.

The app records:

- the roll
- the combat ratio
- enemy Endurance loss
- Lone Wolf Endurance loss
- current weapon and modifiers
- combat outcome

## Mindblast And Immunity

Mindblast is useful, but some enemies ignore it. When a combat preset knows the enemy is immune, the app leaves out the Mindblast bonus.

If you are resolving combat by hand, check the section text before adding Mindblast.

## Evasion

Some fights allow evasion. If the book offers an evasion route, the app can show that route and resolve the required round when needed.

Do not treat evasion as failure. In Book 1, surviving long enough to carry the message can be the smarter win.

## Victory Choices

Some fights do not have a single automatic victory route. When the book gives you a choice after combat, the app records the win and waits for you to choose the route.

## Combat History

Completed fights are archived in combat history. Achievements use that durable history for combat milestones such as first victory and the Gourgaz fight.
